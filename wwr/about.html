History

The Humble Beginnings: Primitive Rafts The story of rafts begins as a tale of necessity. Rafts were initially conceived as basic and reliable means of transportation across bodies of water. Picture early explorers and traders lashing logs or planks together to create these rudimentary vessels. Larger logs could be tethered to form sizeable rafts capable of carrying heavy loads. These primitive rafts served practical purposes—transporting construction materials, agricultural goods, and even livestock. They also allowed humans to venture beyond their immediate surroundings, expanding horizons across rivers and lakes.
